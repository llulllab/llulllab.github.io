Education & Public Engagement

Pedagogy as intervention — rewiring how problems are posed, approached, and reimagined through situated learning environments.

Learning as Intervention

Llull Lab contributes to the wider ecosystem of knowledge through workshops, training programs, and public events that span across disciplines and institutions.

We treat pedagogy not as a separate function, but as a core method of intervention: a way to rewire how problems are posed, approached, and reimagined. Through situated learning environments, we cultivate a shared vocabulary for navigating technological change.

Transparency & Reports

Q Reports

Each quarter, Llull Lab publishes a Q Report — a structured, high-resolution overview of the laboratory's institutional state, research trajectory, and operational priorities.

Q Reports are designed as method-oriented transparency documents, capturing not only what was produced, but how and why specific research directions were pursued. They serve three primary functions: ensuring transparent self-assessment of objectives and outputs, documenting methodological and infrastructural development, and providing strategic insight into the sociotechnical transformations navigated by the laboratory.

Typical contents include: Executive Summary, Organizational Focus and Research Structure, Research Instruments and Platform Updates, Key Metrics (when applicable), and Next-Quarter Objectives.

Scientific articles, peer-reviewed research, and extended theoretical developments are published separately through dedicated publication channels (e.g. Q-PRE). This separation allows Q Reports to remain concise, readable, and strategically focused.

Q Reports may be accompanied by presentations, public discussions, or selective participation in academic and sectoral events, ensuring that Llull Lab's research remains engaged, visible, and responsive.
